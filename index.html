The Red River Gorge is a canyon system located along the Red River in Central-Eastern Kentucky. Most of the Gorge is located within Daniel Boone National Forest and is referred to as the Red River Gorge Geological Area, which is roughly 29,000 acres in size. Comprising of tough terrain made of limestone rock faces and sandstone cliffs, the park can offer a variety of activities such as hiking, climbing, spelunking, camping, and much more. Geologically, the park is very interesting as there are an estimated 150 arches within the park, a number surpassed only by Arches National Park located in Utah, USA.
